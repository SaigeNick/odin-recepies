Varenyky

Description

Step into the world of ultimate comfort with Varenyky, the beloved, pillowy dumplings that are a true cornerstone of Ukrainian and Eastern European cuisine. These tender, half-moon pockets of dough are a labor of love, designed to hold an incredible array of mouthwatering fillings. From classic savory options like creamy mashed potatoes mixed with tangy cheese, earthy wild mushrooms, or braised cabbage, to sweet summer bursts of juicy sour cherries, there is a varenyky variation for every craving. Each bite is a testament to the magic of simple, humble ingredients transformed into something deeply satisfying.

The experience of eating varenyky is only elevated by the irresistible, rich toppings that traditionally dress them. Fresh out of the boiling water, savory dumplings are usually tossed in melted butter, smothered in sweet caramelized onions and crispy bacon bits, and finished with a generous, cooling dollop of sour cream. Making varenyky is also a cherished tradition, often bringing family and friends around the kitchen table to roll, fill, and pinch the dough together. This recipe will guide you step-by-step to mastering these delicate, melt-in-your-mouth dumplings, bringing a taste of authentic Eastern European hospitality straight to your home.

Ingredients

For dough:

  • 400 g flour
  • 200 ml water
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • ⅓ tbsp salt

For dough:

  • 300 g cottage cheese
  • 1 tbsp sugar or honey
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 50 g butter
  • sour cream or honey for serving

Steps

  1. Add 200 ml of water, 2 tablespoons of oil, 1 teaspoon of sugar, and ⅓ teaspoon of salt to 400 g of flour. Mix thoroughly with a spoon first, then knead the dough with your hands. Leave to rest for 10 minutes.
  2. For the filling, mix 300 g of cottage cheese with 1 tablespoon of sugar or honey and 1 egg yolk. Mix thoroughly until the ingredients are combined.
  3. Roll out the dough to a thickness of a couple of millimeters and cut out circles with a glass or a special round mold. Knead the leftover dough into a ball, roll it out again, and cut out the remaining circles.
  4. Place the filling in the dumplings so that they are full enough but stick together well. Each dumpling will require approximately 1-2 teaspoons of filling, depending on the size of the dumplings.
  5. Boil water in a pot and add the dumplings. Cook them for 2-3 minutes after the water boils with the dumplings in it.
  6. Serve the dumplings hot, greased with 50 g of butter, together with sour cream or honey.
