Borscht

Description

Welcome to the heart of Eastern European comfort food: Borscht. This vibrant, ruby-red soup is much more than just a vegetable stew; it is a centuries-old culinary tradition that warms the soul from the inside out. At its core, borscht is a celebration of humble, earthy ingredients—sweet beets, tender cabbage, root vegetables, and a rich broth—slow-simmered to perfection to create a deeply complex flavor profile. Whether you prefer a hearty, meat-based version with tender chunks of beef and pork, or a bright, vegetarian take, this soup is a masterpiece of sweet, sour, and savory balance.

The true magic of borscht lies in how it is served and shared. A steaming bowl is never truly complete without a generous dollop of rich sour cream, a scattering of fresh fragrant dill, and a thick slice of crusty dark rye bread rubbed with raw garlic on the side. Every spoonful offers a taste of home and history, and it famously tastes even better the next day as the ingredients continue to meld. Dive into this recipe to bring a pot of this iconic, jewel-toned soup into your own kitchen and experience the ultimate comfort in a bowl.

Ingredients

  • 1 carrot
  • 1 beet
  • 200 gr Cabbage
  • 2-3 potatoes
  • 1 onion
  • 300-400 gr meat(any)
  • 1.5-2 l Water

Steps

  1. First, cook the broth. Pour 1.5-2 liters of water into a pot. Add the meat and place over medium heat. Skim off the foam before it boils. Once the broth boils, cover with a lid and simmer for an hour and a half.
  2. Meanwhile, prepare the roux. Peel the beets, carrots, and onions. Grate the beets on a coarse grater and the carrots on a medium grater. Dice the onions.
  3. Heat the oil in a frying pan over medium heat, add the onions and carrots, and fry for 5 minutes. Then add the beetroot (you can sprinkle it with citric acid or sprinkle it with fresh lemon juice – this will make the borscht really red). Fry the vegetables for another 5 minutes, add the tomato paste, stir and fry for another 5-7 minutes.
  4. Now let's cook the borscht itself. Add the chopped potatoes to the broth, salt to taste, then add the cabbage and bay leaf. When the potatoes are almost ready (after 10-15 minutes), add the roux. Cook for another 5-10 minutes until ready.
  5. Serve the borscht with sour cream and herbs.
    Bon appetit!
